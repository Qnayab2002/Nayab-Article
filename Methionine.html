Methionine and Cysteine

Methionine and Cysteine are the two amino acids that contain sulfur. These amino acids play crucial roles in human health and biological functions. Sulfur-containing amino acids are important in protein synthesis, enzyme activity, and detoxification processes.

List of Sulfur-Containing Amino Acids

  • Methionine (Met): An essential amino acid that serves as a methyl group donor and is involved in the initiation of protein synthesis.
  • Cysteine (Cys): A semi-essential amino acid that plays a key role in maintaining protein structure via disulfide bonds and in antioxidant activity.
