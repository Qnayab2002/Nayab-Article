Anaerobic Glycolysis

Anaerobic Glycolysis (Fermentation) ATP synthesis without oxygen occurs in the process of anaerobic glycolysis, also known as fermentation. This process takes place in the cytosol of cells and involves the breakdown of glucose to produce ATP, without the use of oxygen. In anaerobic glycolysis, glucose is converted into pyruvate, which is then converted into lactic acid or ethanol, depending on the type of cell.
